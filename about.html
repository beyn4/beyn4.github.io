About Nadia

I am an alumna of Duke University with a degree in biology and global health and a minor in computational biology. At Duke, I was a University Scholar, Margolis Scholar and QuestBridge Scholar. I graduated with distinction with a thesis titled Telemedicine utilization by North Carolina farmworkers: a content analysis. I am now pursuing an MPH in health policy at George Washington University.

During my tenure at Duke, I researched the use of language in prescription drug ads, equitable vaccine distribution, telehealth use by farmworkers and people with musculoskeletal conditions, and social determinants of health in North Carolina's Triangle region. I also co-taught "Durham Middle School Outreach," an undergraduate service-learning seminar on education sociology, for two years.

I served as managing editor of The Chronicle, Duke's student newspaper. My work there has been recognized by the North Carolina Open Government Coalition and the Associated Collegiate Press. I was also co-president of Duke's chapter of Her Campus.

In summer 2022, I interned on the North Carolina investigations desk for McClatchy News and covered health and policy for The Charlotte Observer. I'm a 2022-23 ProPublica Emerging Reporter fellow and was a 2021 recipient of the organization's Diversity Scholarship. I've been quoted in the New York Times and had my reporting cited in the Washington Post.

I was also books editor of teen-led publication Affinity Magazine, facilitating and writing book reviews for middle grade through adult books. My reviews have been quoted on Amazon and social media.
