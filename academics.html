As a University Scholar and Margolis Scholar at Duke, the majority of my endeavors have been interdisciplinary. My primary interests are in human health, data, communication and education.

Teaching

HOUSECS 59, DPS Middle School Outreach — Spring 2021, Spring 2022

This undergraduate-led seminar focuses on the sociology of education and has a service-learning component wherein students volunteer at a Durham middle school. My reflections from spring 2021 can be found in this Twitter thread.

Projects

Spatial Determinants of Health / HealthCarolina

In 2021, I became intrigued by access to health facilities and resources in North Carolina, inspired by an online tool created by my home county. Over the summer, I used Python to find the average and median distance between residences and health facilities by neighborhood area, and the visualization can be found here. I did a short talk on spatial access to care during the Margolis internship here. In fall 2021, I collaborated with other students in my COMPSCI 316 course to develop a web app that categorized health statistics by county. The repository for that project is here and the online version here.

Research

Margolis Summer Internship 2021

I conducted a literature review at the intersection of musculoskeletal health and telemedicine as part of the Kate B. Reynolds telehealth disparities project team. I presented at the virtual summer research symposium in July, and my presentation can be found here.

Vaccine Equity Project

In spring 2021, I was part of a team that developed policy recommendations for COVID-19 vaccine distribution from a feminist perspective based on historical case studies. I co-wrote a group report that is now online via Revaluing Care in the Global Economy.

Explicit and Implicit Language in Prescription Drug Ads

In spring and summer 2020, I examined the use of language and visuals in prescription drug ads with the Medical Cognition Lab. The presentation of information in these ads can have substantial effects on viewer comprehension.

Presentations

Communicating Toward Health Justice, University Scholars Program Symposium. March 13, 2021.

I discussed the importance of health communication at the annual USP symposium, which convenes undergraduate, graduate and professional students as well as faculty to discuss topics surrounding an annual theme. The theme of the 2021 symposium was "Wicked Problems/Wicked Solutions", which refers to complex dilemmas with multiple possible approaches.
